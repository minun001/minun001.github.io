Overview

One research identity across mobility, energy, and public-facing AI

The common thread in my work is building models that stay useful when they leave the paper and enter a real environment. That means predictive systems for road behavior, adaptive forecasting for energy operations, and language-based reasoning tools that support clearer decisions.

Mobility intelligence

I study autonomous driving and traffic analysis with an emphasis on anticipating motion, understanding road context, and building systems that improve safety in dynamic environments.

Energy forecasting

My forecasting work focuses on photovoltaic generation and scalable management, connecting time-series modeling to operating conditions that matter in practice.

LLM applications

I use language models as structured reasoning tools for legal and safety-oriented domains where traceability and decision support matter as much as raw prediction quality.
